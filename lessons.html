Nature of Communication

Origin of the word "communication"
Came from the latin words "communis" and "communicare" .

Communis means to make "common".
Communicare means to make common to many, share.

What is Communication?
It is a systematic process of sending and receiving messages.
The sharing of information between two people or more.
It is dynamic, ongoing and ever changing.

The Nature of Communication
Systemic Process- Communication occurs in a set of systems wherein it's difficult to tell when it will begin or end.

People Interaction- Communication cannot occur without a sender and a receiver. In short, It cannot happen with one person alone.

Exchange of Verbal and Nonverbal Messages- Anything that you do or say sends out a message.

Creation and Interpretation of Meanings- Based on a communicator's past experiences, beliefs, values, goals, self concept, and cultural orientation, meanings can be created.

Elements of Communication
Sender- This is the encoder of the message.
Receiver- He or she is the one that receives and decodes the messages.
Encoding- is the act of putting in the message in the appropriate channel or medium.
Decoding- is the act of interpreting and creating meanings.
Message- This is what is being sent through verbal or non-verbal means to the receiver.
Channel- It refers to the means of which communication is transmitted.
Feedback- It is the returning message in response to the intial one
